Music and Skateboarding

by Jared Wilber · September 2017

Note - if you're on mobile the visualization won't properly line up with text - sorry!

I grew up in San Diego, California, and, like many other kids from Southern California, skateboarding was a huge part of my childhood. One of the biggest draws to skateboarding for me was the music associated with it. It was different then what they played on the radio. It felt counterculture. It felt exclusive. It felt cool.

Of course I was in no way unique with this sentiment - skateboarding and music have been intrinsically linked since the inception of skateboarding in the 1950s and 1960s, with many artists (e.g. Morrissey, Black Flag, Dinosaur Jr. etc.) having become heavily associated with skate culture.

And therein lives the purpose of this blog post:

Which music artists and genres are featued the most across the space of skateboarding?

In the visualization below, each node corresponds to a separate artist, with size encoding the number of times that artist has been used in skate videos. The larger the area of circle, the more popular is the artist in skateboarding. Each node is color-coded by music genre. The toggle options allow you to arrange the data by various levels.

Hover over each node to get artist specific information.

Data and Methodology

The data was scraped from skatevideosite.com. I wrote a quick Python script to grab the soundtracks for the top 2,000 most-viewed skate videos, and then manually removed very niche, largely unknown videos (e.g. videos from Brazil). After this step, the data was cleaned and formatted. Genres for the artists were obtained via the Spotify API.

To produce the visualizations, I used Javascript's amazing D3 libary. In particular, I used the library's force-layout to produce the bubble-chart. This visualization was inspired by work from the New York Times and Jim Vallandingham, and the code is available in my Github.
