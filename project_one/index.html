Music and Skateboarding

by Jared Wilber · September 2017

Skateboarding and music have been intrinsically linked since the inception of skateboarding in the 1950s and 1960s. While this relationship manifests itself in a number of different ways, nowhere is this connection made more explicit than skate videos.

Growing up, a large number of my favorite skate video parts were a product of the music being skated to, rather than the skating itself. I doubt that I'm alone in this sentiment.

Music selection can make or break video parts. Some video parts, like Jason Dill's in Photosynthesis or Mark Appleyard's in Flip's Sorry, have a great combination of both music and skating. Others, like Matt Beach's in Can't Stop, don't. Maybe I'm crazy, but part of me still things he would have had a successful career had his debut part not been set to his friends jam sesh. Oh well.

So what songs do skaters like to skate to? Morrissey? Dinosaur Jr.? Andre Nickatina? Explore below to find out.

Data and Methodology

The data was scraped from skatevideosite.com. I wrote a quick Python script to grab the soundtracks for the top 2,000 most-viewed skate videos, and then manually removed very niche, largely unknown videos (e.g. videos from Brazil). After this step, the data was cleaned and formatted. Genres for the artists were obtained via the Spotify API.

To produce the visualizations, I used Javascript's amazing D3 libary. In particular, I used the library's force-layout to produce the bubble-chart. This visualization was inspired by work from the New York Times and Jim Vallandingham, and the code is available in my Github.
